Red Flags

DM / Text — Generic Opener

Hey [Name], I noticed [Business Name] doesn't have a website yet. I help local businesses get online fast — clean, professional sites starting at $300. Would love to show you what I can do. Interested?

Instagram / Facebook — Salons & Barbers

Love what you do at [Business Name]. Quick question — do you have a website where people can book appointments? I build sites for local salons starting at $300, usually ready in about a week. Happy to send some examples if you're curious.

In-Person / Phone

Hey, I'm KP — I do freelance web design for small businesses locally. I help businesses like yours get a professional website up fast, starting at $300. Most clients start getting calls from it within the first month. Do you have a website right now?
"I can just use Wix or Squarespace."
You absolutely can — and some people do. But most business owners are too busy running their business to learn a website builder and keep it updated. You're not paying for a website. You're paying to never think about it again. I handle the build, the setup, and I'm there if something needs changing.
"It's too expensive right now."
That's fair — and it's actually why this makes sense now. If your site brings in even two new customers a month, it pays for itself in the first 30 days. I also split it in half — 50% to start, 50% when it goes live. So you're only putting down $[half] today.
"I already have someone working on it."
No problem at all — keep my info. I turn projects around fast and I'm always transparent about pricing. If it ever doesn't work out, reach out and I'll take care of you. No pressure.

3 Days After First Message

Hey [Name], just checking in — did my last message reach you? No pressure at all. Happy to send over some examples of sites I've built if that helps you get a feel for my work.

1 Week Later — Final

Last one from me — I know you're busy. If a website ever becomes a priority, I'm here. I usually book out 1–2 weeks ahead so just reach out when you're ready. Wishing you a great week.

Closing the Deal

Perfect. So the total is $[amount]. I ask for 50% upfront — $[half] — to lock in your spot and get started, then the other half when the site goes live. I take Venmo, Cash App, Zelle, or PayPal. Want to get started?

After Payment Received

Payment received — thank you. I'll send over a quick form to collect your info (logo, photos, colors, services) and we'll be off. Target completion: [date]. Excited to build something great for [Business Name].
These scripts are for when a client likes the work but pushes back on price. Never drop below your floor price.

They Want a Discount

I appreciate you asking — I don't have much flexibility on price because my rates already reflect the actual time it takes to do good work. What I can do is adjust what's included. If we drop [feature], I can bring it down to $[lower]. Does that work, or would you rather keep everything and stick with the original?

They Want More for the Same Price

I want to make sure I deliver real quality, which means I need to keep the scope tight for this budget. If you want to add [extra feature], I can do that for an additional $[price]. Otherwise I'll focus on doing the agreed scope really well — which is going to get you results on its own.

Contract / Retainer Discount

If you want to lock in 3 months of retainer upfront, I'll give you 10% off — so instead of $450 over three months, it's $405. You get stability on your end, I get consistency on mine. Want to do that?
Red flags = charge more or walk away. These signals mean the project will cost you more in time, stress, and revisions than the quote suggests.
"We'll figure out the details as we go." Vague scope always expands. Get every deliverable in writing before taking a deposit.
"My nephew said he could do it for $50." Price-anchoring with a low-ball comparison. Respond with value, not defensiveness. If they keep pushing, they're not your client.
"Can you just do a quick change?" (×5) Death by a thousand favors. Log every "quick" request in the Scope Creep Tracker. After the 2nd free change, start billing.
"I need it done by Friday." (It's Wednesday.) Rush fee applies. Always. 30% minimum. Do not let urgency pressure you into dropping your rate.
"I'll know what I want when I see it." No defined brief = unlimited revisions. Require a written brief or Canva mockup approval before building anything.
"Can we do the payment after it's done?" Never. 50% upfront is non-negotiable. No deposit = no protection. If they refuse, walk.
They're rude, dismissive, or hard to reach in the sales process. That behavior triples when they're a paying client. Trust your gut. Problem clients cost more than they're worth.
